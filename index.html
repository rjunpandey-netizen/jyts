How to Use
Daily routine and trading rules
Learning Refreshers
What is SMA250?SMA250 is the 250-day average price of the ASX 200. Think of it as the market long-term mood. When the ASX is above it, markets are healthy (GEAR). Below it, markets are weak (BBOZ).
What is RSI?RSI (Relative Strength Index) measures how fast prices are moving. Above 70 = overbought. Below 30 = oversold. Between 40-60 = normal momentum.
Why GEAR and BBOZ?GEAR rises 2x when ASX rises. BBOZ rises 2x when ASX falls. These let you profit in both up and down markets, but losses are also amplified.
Drawdowns are normal.Even good strategies lose money for months. The key is conviction in your rules. Never exit a strategy during a drawdown unless your rules say to.
Your edge: SMA250.If the ASX is above SMA250 and you hold GEAR, over the long run you capture the uptrend. Simple edges outlast complex ones.
Position sizing matters.Being 80% in GEAR vs 40% in GEAR makes an enormous difference over time. Follow the allocation, not your gut.
Mean reversion vs momentum.Momentum = buy what is going up. Mean reversion = buy when it has pulled back. Your system combines both.
Paper trade first, always.Your brain needs to experience drawdowns emotionally before risking real money. 3 months minimum, aim for 6.
What is a drawdown?A drawdown is how much your portfolio has fallen from its peak. They are temporary if your strategy is sound.
Play your own game.Build conviction in rules you understand. If you do not understand why a rule works, you will abandon it when it matters most.
